Christian Blanton

Gameplay/Graphics Engineer

Unstoppaball

Unstoppaball is a 2D sidescrolling-platformer with high octane smashing and platforming!

A collaboration with one other engineer and a 3-man audio team; team of 5 people.
Unity, Perforce, C#

Trailer

My Contributions

Programming

  • Implemented physics-based platforming abilities including grappling hook, grappling pull, jump, dash, and helicopter float for core movement gameplay.
  • Created 2D-sprite fragmentation scripts to give the impression of objects exploding upon impact.
  • Coded scripts so characters would properly play animations according to their current state.
  • Optimized performance be deleting any debris or particles that went off screen.
  • Implemented level navigation UI.

Level Design

  • Designed unique level layouts that eased the player into learning new abilities by ramping up difficulty in destroying buildings and increasing building density.
  • Challenged the player to play without the jump ability on unique water level to encourage using other abilities.

Technical Art

  • Created the majority of the pixel art and animations in the game through Aseprite.
  • Achieved a parallax background effect for each level to give sense of speed.
  • Implemented particle systems to explosive crates, water towers, and fire hydrants to add explosions and water effects.
  • Made reflective, rippling water using Shader Graph.
  • Rendered the chain of the main character using TrailRenderer.
  • Recorded gameplay and released trailer for the game in Premiere Pro.

Other

  • Led and scheduled meetings between engineering and audio team.
  • Gave feedback and direction on audio to suit the game. Integrated such audio into the product.
